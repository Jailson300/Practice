Introduction

The Meitei tribe is renowned for its deep-rooted traditions, which are intricately woven into the fabric of daily life. Central to these is Sanamahism, the indigenous religion of the Meitei people, which honors the elements of nature and the ancestors. The tribe celebrates numerous festivals, with Lai Haraoba being the most significant, a grand event that pays homage to the deities and retells the creation of the universe through dance, music, and rituals.The Meitei language, or Meiteilon, is one of the oldest languages in India and is recognized for its rich literary heritage. The tribe's oral traditions, poetry, and classical texts are cherished treasures, reflecting the wisdom and creativity of the Meitei people. The traditional script, Meitei Mayek, has seen a revival in recent years, symbolizing the tribe's commitment to preserving its cultural identity.The Meitei tribe is celebrated for its exceptional artistry, particularly in weaving and handicrafts. The traditional attire, such as Phanek and Innaphi, showcases intricate designs and vibrant colors, reflecting the skill and aesthetic sensibilities of Meitei artisans. Pung Cholom, the drum dance, and the classical dance form Ras Leela are world-renowned, capturing the essence of Meitei spirituality and cultural expression.
